# Deep Research Methodology

Compiled from deer-flow deep-research skill. Systematic multi-angle research for any topic requiring web research or content generation.

---

## Core Principle

**Never generate content based solely on general knowledge.** A single search query is NEVER enough. Search from at least 3-5 different angles before synthesizing.

---

## Phase 1: Broad Exploration

1. **Initial Survey** — Search the main topic to understand overall context
2. **Identify Dimensions** — From results, identify key subtopics, themes, and angles
3. **Map the Territory** — Note different perspectives, stakeholders, and viewpoints

Example:
```
Topic: "AI in healthcare"
Searches: "AI healthcare applications 2024", "healthcare AI market trends"

Identified dimensions:
- Diagnostic AI (radiology, pathology)
- Treatment recommendation systems
- Regulatory landscape
- Ethical considerations
```

---

## Phase 2: Deep Dive

For each important dimension:

1. **Specific Queries** — Search with precise keywords for each subtopic
2. **Multiple Phrasings** — Try different keyword combinations
3. **Fetch Full Content** — Read important sources in full, not just snippets
4. **Follow References** — When sources mention other resources, search for those too

---

## Phase 3: Diversity & Validation

Seek diverse information types for comprehensive coverage:

| Information Type | Purpose | Search Keywords |
|-----------------|---------|-----------------|
| **Facts & Data** | Concrete evidence | "statistics", "data", "market size" |
| **Examples & Cases** | Real-world applications | "case study", "implementation" |
| **Expert Opinions** | Authority perspectives | "expert analysis", "interview" |
| **Trends & Predictions** | Future direction | "trends [year]", "forecast" |
| **Comparisons** | Context and alternatives | "vs", "comparison", "alternatives" |
| **Challenges** | Balanced view | "challenges", "limitations", "criticism" |

---

## Phase 4: Synthesis Check

Before generating content, verify:

- [ ] Searched from at least 3-5 different angles
- [ ] Fetched and read the most important sources in full
- [ ] Have concrete data, examples, and expert perspectives
- [ ] Explored both positive aspects and challenges/limitations
- [ ] Information is current and from authoritative sources

**If any answer is NO, continue researching.**

---

## Search Strategy Tips

### Effective Query Patterns

```
# Be specific with context
❌ "AI trends"
✅ "enterprise AI adoption trends 2024"

# Include authoritative source hints
"[topic] research paper"
"[topic] McKinsey report"
"[topic] industry analysis"

# Search for specific content types
"[topic] case study"
"[topic] statistics"
"[topic] expert interview"
```

### Temporal Awareness

Always use the actual current year in searches:

| User Intent | Precision Needed | Example |
|---|---|---|
| "today / just released" | Month + Day | "tech news February 28 2026" |
| "this week" | Week range | "releases week of Feb 24 2026" |
| "recently / latest" | Month | "AI breakthroughs February 2026" |
| "this year / trends" | Year | "software trends 2026" |

---

## Quality Bar

Research is sufficient when you can answer:
- What are the key facts and data points?
- What are 2-3 concrete real-world examples?
- What do experts say about this topic?
- What are the current trends and future directions?
- What are the challenges or limitations?

---

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Stopping after 1-2 searches
- ❌ Relying on snippets without reading full sources
- ❌ Searching only one aspect of a multi-faceted topic
- ❌ Ignoring contradicting viewpoints
- ❌ Using outdated information when current data exists
- ❌ Starting content generation before research is complete
